 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="right"><B>Exhibit 1.01 </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:12pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>National Oilwell Varco, Inc. </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:12pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Conflict Minerals Report </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center">For the reporting period from January&nbsp;1, 2014 to December&nbsp;31, 2014 </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">This Conflict Minerals Report of National Oilwell Varco, Inc. (the &#147;Company&#148;, &#147;NOV&#148;, &#147;we&#148;, &#147;us&#148; or &#147;our&#148;)
for the calendar year ended December&nbsp;31, 2014 is presented pursuant to Rule 13p-1 under the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 (the &#147;Rule&#148;). The Rule imposes certain reporting obligations on issuers whose manufactured products include
cassiterite, columbite-tantalite, gold, wolframite, and their derivatives, which are limited to tin, tantalum, tungsten, and gold (the &#147;Designated Minerals&#148;), where such Designated Minerals are necessary to the functionality or production
of such products. If any Designated Minerals necessary to functionality or production of such products originated from the Democratic Republic of the Congo or an adjoining country that shares an internationally recognized border with the Democratic
Republic of the Congo (the &#147;Conflict Region&#148;) and are not from recycled or scrap sources, the Rule imposes certain additional due diligence and reporting obligations. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Product Description </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">This Conflict Minerals Report
covers all products manufactured by NOV or contracted to be manufactured for NOV. Those products include, but are not limited to, those listed in <U>Attachment A</U> to this Conflict Minerals Report. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Conflict Minerals Policy </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">NOV&#146;s policy is to
develop a &#147;conflict-free&#148; supply chain &#150; one that does not use Designated Minerals sourced from mines or smelters that have directly or indirectly aided armed groups in the Conflict Region. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">A copy of our policy can be found at: http://investors.nov.com/phoenix.zhtml?c=97690&amp;p=irol-govconduct. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Reasonable Country of Origin Inquiry </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Our conflict
minerals compliance program was initiated by a review of our product portfolio. In 2013, our engineers reviewed the raw materials and/or purchased subcomponents used in our products to determine which items contained Designated Minerals that are
necessary to a product&#146;s functionality or production. Since the initial review in 2013, our engineers have similarly reviewed new raw materials and subcomponents as they are added to our purchasing systems. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">NOV internally developed a computerized Conflict Minerals Management System (the &#147;CMM System&#148;) that provides a platform for conflict minerals
compliance by all NOV business units impacted by conflict minerals. The CMM System compares the list of materials and subcomponents identified by our engineers as containing Designated Minerals against purchase order items received by NOV during the
calendar year. The CMM System identifies suppliers that delivered items containing Designated Minerals. The identified suppliers are directed to complete an online Reasonable Country of Origin Inquiry (&#147;RCOI&#148;). The purpose of our CMM
System is to determine which suppliers should complete an RCOI, and further to identify suppliers requiring additional Due Diligence (see &#147;Due Diligence&#148; below) based on the RCOI responses. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">During the 2014 calendar year, we asked approximately 700 suppliers to complete an online RCOI through our CMM System. The survey response rate was 17%. Of
the suppliers who responded, no supplier reported having sourced from the Conflict Region. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Due Diligence </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Our due diligence process is designed in accordance with Annex I of the Organization for Economic Co-Operation and Development (&#147;OECD&#148;) Due
Diligence Guidance for Responsible Supply Chains of Minerals from Conflict-Affected and High-Risk Areas (the &#147;OECD Framework&#148;). All steps taken by NOV in preparing this Conflict Minerals Report are in accordance with the OECD Framework.
</P>

<p Style='page-break-before:always'>
<HR  SIZE="3" style="COLOR:#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">
 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Our due diligence activities generally included follow-up questions with the relevant suppliers or their
sub-suppliers within the supply chain, a review of any mine or smelter certifications, a review of materials spec sheets, and similar activities designed to determine whether raw materials and/or purchased subcomponents used in our products contain
Designated Minerals, the source of any Designated Minerals, and whether that source directly or indirectly aids armed groups in the Conflict Region. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">This
Conflict Minerals Report was <I><U>not</U></I> subjected to an independent private sector audit. </P>

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Artificial Lift Systems </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Blenders </P>
