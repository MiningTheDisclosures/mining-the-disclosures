<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Rockwell Automation&#146;s supply chain is complex and there are multiple tiers between the Company and the mine. Rockwell Automation relies on its suppliers
to provide information on the origin of the tin, tantalum, tungsten and gold (3TG)&nbsp;contained in components that are included in Rockwell Automation products. </P> <P STYLE="font-size:18pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>

<TD WIDTH="4%" VALIGN="top" ALIGN="left"><B>II.</B></TD>
<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"><B>Reasonable Country of Origin Inquiry </B></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>OECD Step 1 &#150; Establish strong company management systems
</I></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">As a part of the development and execution of Rockwell Automation&#146;s conflict minerals compliance program, the Company incorporated leading
practices from multi-industry initiatives such as MAPI (Manufacturers Alliance for Productivity and Innovation), NEMA (National Electrical Manufactures Association), EICC (Electronic Industry Citizen Coalition) and GeSI (Global <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">e-Sustainability</FONT> Initiative). </P>

<p Style='page-break-before:always'>
<HR  SIZE="3" style="COLOR:#999999" WIDTH="100%" ALIGN="CENTER">

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Rockwell Automation utilizes an executive steering committee, and relevant staff functions and technical experts
(e.g. Legal, Purchasing, Strategic Sourcing, Chemistry&nbsp;&amp; Materials Engineering) to support the Company&#146;s Conflict Minerals program managed by the Engineering Services Project Management Office (Conflict Minerals Team). </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Rockwell Automation communicated conflict minerals expectations to suppliers and the public via a Conflict Minerals Supply Chain Statement, which is available
at the following internet website: <U><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">http://www.rockwellautomation.com/rockwellautomation/about-us/sustainability-ethics/integrity-compliance-overview.page</FONT></U>? (Rockwell Automation Conflict Minerals Supply
Chain Statement April 2015) </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Rockwell Automation adopted a conflict minerals transparency contract provision that has been incorporated into purchase
orders and new contracts and is inserted into existing contracts as they are renewed. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Employees and suppliers can report violations of Rockwell
Automation&#146;s policies at <U><FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">http://www.rockwellautomation.com/rockwellautomation/about-us/sustainability-ethics/ombudsman.page</FONT></U>? </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Existing Rockwell Automation processes for risk mitigation and record retention will be utilized as required. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In 2013 Rockwell Automation purchased a data management tool to maintain conflict minerals information provided by suppliers. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Companies being evaluated as new suppliers to RA are subjected to a New Supplier Audit process. Questions added in 2014 to this process are used to determine
a supplier&#146;s conflict minerals engagement. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>OECD Step 2 &#150; Identify and assess risk in the supply chain </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Because of the complexity and size of its supply chain, Rockwell Automation developed a risk-based approach that focused on direct suppliers that it believed
were likely to provide Rockwell Automation with components and raw materials containing tin, tantalum, tungsten and gold (3TG). </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Rockwell Automation
intends to use its suppliers to influence their suppliers and/or smelters and refiners of 3TG. The smelters and refiners are consolidation points for raw ore and are in the best position in the total supply chain to know the origin of the ores. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Rockwell Automation provided training materials on its conflict minerals policies to internal Rockwell Automation employees (e.g. supplier quality, corporate
purchasing, strategic sourcing, sales, design, design support organizations and compliance personnel), and to suppliers as part of its conflict minerals request for information. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">To identify sources of 3TG in its products Rockwell Automation: </P> <P STYLE="font-size:6pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>
<TABLE STYLE="BORDER-COLLAPSE:COLLAPSE; font-family:Times New Roman; font-size:10pt" BORDER="0" CELLPADDING="0" CELLSPACING="0" WIDTH="100%">

<TD WIDTH="4%" VALIGN="top" ALIGN="left"><B>III.</B></TD>
<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"><B>Due Diligence Design and Performance </B></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>Due Diligence Design </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Rockwell Automation&#146;s due diligence processes have been designed in conformance with the due diligence related steps of the framework in The Organization
for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) Due Diligence Guidance for Responsible Supply Chains of Minerals from Conflict-Affected and High-Risk Areas: Second Edition (OECD 2013), and related Supplements for gold, tantalum, tin and tungsten
(OECD Framework). </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>Due Diligence Measures Performed </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><I>OECD Step 3 &#150; Design and implement a strategy to respond to identified risks </I></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Rockwell Automation supported audits of 3TG smelters and refiners conducted by third parties through its participation in, and payment of membership dues to
the Conflict Free Smelter Initiative. Rockwell Automation became a member of the Conflict Free Sourcing Initiative in 2014. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Suppliers were evaluated
based on their smelter and refiner information obtained from Conflict Minerals Materials Template (CMRT) declarations. If supplier information indicated they used 3TG from the DRC region, Rockwell Automation used the Conflict Free Sourcing
Initiative and general public information to determine a smelter or refiner&#146;s conflict minerals status. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Although there was no instance where it was
necessary to implement risk mitigation or any corrective actions to suppliers in 2014, Rockwell Automation&#146;s risk mitigation efforts would include the following steps if a smelters or refiners were identified as supporting rebel activity. </P>
