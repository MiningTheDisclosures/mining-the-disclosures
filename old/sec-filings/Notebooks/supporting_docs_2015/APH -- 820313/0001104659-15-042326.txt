<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">Item 1.01 Conflict Minerals Disclosure and Report</font></b></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Amphenol Corporation (together with its subsidiaries, the &#147;Company&#148; or &#147;Amphenol&#148;) evaluated its product lines and determined that with respect to certain products the Company manufactures or contracts to manufacture, tin, tungsten, tantalum and/or gold (&#147;3TG&#148;) are necessary to the functionality or production of such products.&#160; The Company conducted in good faith a reasonable country of origin inquiry and performed appropriate due diligence designed to determine whether any 3TG originated from minerals coming from the Democratic Republic of the Congo (the &#147;DRC&#148;) or any country that shares an internationally recognized border with the DRC, or are from recycled or scrap sources, and whether the Company&#146;s procurement directly or indirectly finances or benefits armed conflict in the DRC or an adjoining country.&#160; Through the Company&#146;s reasonable country of origin inquiry and due diligence process, it determined that a portion of our supply chain is DRC conflict undeterminable.&#160; Accordingly, the Company has prepared a Conflict Minerals Report.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">A copy of the Company&#146;s Conflict Minerals Report is provided as Exhibit&nbsp;1.01 hereto and is publicly available at: www.amphenol.com by clicking on &#147;Investors&#148;, then &#147;Governance&#148;, then &#147;Conflict Minerals&#148;.&#160; The information contained on our website is not incorporated by reference into this Form SD or our Conflict Minerals Report and should not be considered part of this Form SD or the Conflict Minerals Report.&#160; The Conflict Minerals Report sets forth the reasonable country of origin inquiry and related due diligence undertaken by the Company.</font></p>

<p align="center" style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;text-align:center;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">For The Year Ended December&nbsp;31, 2014</font></b></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">This report (this &#147;Conflict Minerals Report&#148;) for the year ended December&nbsp;31, 2014 is presented to comply with Rule&nbsp;13p-1 under the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, as amended, and Form SD (collectively, the &#147;Rule&#148;). The Rule&nbsp;was adopted by the Securities and Exchange Commission (the &#147;SEC&#148;) to implement reporting and disclosure requirements related to certain minerals as directed by the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act of 2010 (the &#147;Dodd-Frank Act&#148;). The Rule&nbsp;imposes certain reporting obligations on SEC registrants, like Amphenol Corporation (together with its subsidiaries, the &#147;Company&#148;, &#147;Amphenol&#148; &#147;us&#148; or &#147;we&#148;), whose manufactured products contain certain minerals or their derivatives which are necessary to the functionality or production of their products. These minerals include cassiterite, columbite-tantalite, gold, wolframite, and their derivatives, which are limited to tin, tantalum, tungsten, and gold (&#147;3TG&#148; or &#147;conflict minerals&#148;) for the purposes of this assessment. These reporting obligations apply to registrants regardless of the geographic origin of the 3TG and whether or not they fund armed conflict in the Democratic Republic of the Congo (the &#147;DRC&#148;) or an adjoining country (together with the DRC, the &#147;Covered Countries&#148; or the &#147;Conflict Region&#148;).</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">This Conflict Minerals Report is not audited.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">This Conflict Minerals Report has been prepared by management of the Company.&#160; The information includes the activities of all majority-owned subsidiaries.</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">1. Company Overview</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Amphenol is one of the world&#146;s largest designers, manufacturers and marketers of electrical, electronic and fiber optic connectors, interconnect systems, antennas, sensors and sensor-based products and coaxial and high-speed specialty cable.&nbsp; Amphenol designs, manufactures and assembles its products at facilities in the Americas, Europe, Asia, Australia and Africa and sells its products through its own global sales force, independent representatives and a global network of electronics distributors.&nbsp; Amphenol has a diversified presence as a leader in high growth areas of the interconnect market including:&nbsp; automotive, broadband, commercial aerospace,&nbsp;industrial,&nbsp;information technology and data communications, military, mobile devices and mobile networks.&#160; </font><font size="2" style="font-size:10.0pt;">Amphenol Corporation was incorporated in Delaware in 1986, but certain businesses now part of the Company have had operations since prior to 1900. Our principal executive offices are located at 358 Hall Avenue, Wallingford, Connecticut, 06492.</font></p>

<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt 1.0in;"><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;">&nbsp;</font></p>
<p style="margin:0in 0in .0001pt 1.0in;"><b><font size="2" face="Times New Roman" style="font-size:10.0pt;font-weight:bold;">POLICY</font></b></p>

To that end, Amphenol is committed to ensuring its products are &#147;DRC conflict-free&#148;, that is, they do not contain metals derived from &#147;conflict minerals&#148;&#160; -&#160; columbite-tantalite (tantalum), cassiterite (tin), gold, wolframite (tungsten) - which directly or indirectly finance or benefit armed groups through mining or mineral trading in the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) or an adjoining country.</font></p>
