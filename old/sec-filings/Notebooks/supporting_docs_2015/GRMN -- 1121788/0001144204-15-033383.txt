<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">This Report for the reporting period beginning
on January 1, 2014 and ending on December 31, 2014 has been prepared pursuant to Rule 13p-1 (the &ldquo;SEC Rule&rdquo;) under
the Securities Exchange Act of 1934, as amended.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

tantalum, tin and tungsten (collectively, the &ldquo;Covered Minerals&rdquo;).</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">Garmin supports an industry-wide approach to addressing social
responsibility issues throughout the supply chain. As part of this approach, Garmin is a paying and active member of the Electronic
Industry Citizenship Coalition (&ldquo;EICC&rdquo;). Garmin collaborates with others in industry through its participation in the
Conflict Free Sourcing Initiative (&ldquo;CFSI&rdquo;), which evolved from the EICC-Global e-Sustainability Initiative (&ldquo;EICC-GeSI&rdquo;).</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">The CFSI is an industry-wide initiative to develop control systems
regarding smelters and refiners through independently validated audits under CFSI&rsquo;s Conflict-Free Smelter Program (&ldquo;CFSP&rdquo;).
Through the CFSP, the CFSI identifies smelters and refiners that produce conflict-free materials. In order to confirm that status,
CFSI uses specially trained independent third-party auditors to verify that these smelters and refiners can be deemed conflict-free.
CFSI publishes online a list of smelters and refiners that meet the standards of the audit.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">In addition, the CFSI developed the Conflict Minerals Reporting
Template (the &ldquo;CMRT&rdquo;) to standardize the collection and transfer of information through the supply chain regarding
mineral country of origin and the identity of smelters and refiners being utilized.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0"><I>Identify and Assess Risk in the Supply Chain</I></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">In 2014, Garmin identified 718 first-tier suppliers that provide
products to Garmin that contain or might reasonably be expected to contain one or more of the Covered Minerals. Garmin also identified
102 manufacturers who supply products that contain or might reasonably be expected to contain one or more of the Covered Minerals
to Garmin or to suppliers from whom Garmin purchases such products. Garmin sent a cover letter to those suppliers and manufacturers
educating them about the SEC Rule. In this cover letter Garmin also requested that the recipients complete the CMRT, including
all smelter/refiner information for any of the Covered Minerals contained in their products.<B> </B></P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0">&nbsp;</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">Garmin determined that it had reason to
believe that Covered Minerals included in its products may have originated in the Democratic Republic of Congo or an adjoining
country (each, a &ldquo;Covered Country&rdquo;) or are not from recycled or scrap sources. Therefore, in accordance with the SEC
Rule, Garmin undertook due diligence measures on the source and chain of custody of Covered Minerals included in its products,
as described below.</P>

<P STYLE="font: 10pt Times New Roman, Times, Serif; margin: 0pt 0; text-align: justify">&nbsp;</P>

in conformance with the due diligence related steps of the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (&ldquo;OECD&rdquo;)
Due Diligence Guidance for Responsible Supply Chains of Minerals from Conflict-Affected and High- Risk Areas, as further set forth
in the specific guidance for downstream companies contained in the supplements on tin, tantalum, tungsten and gold (the &ldquo;OECD
