 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Conflict Minerals Report &#150; May 29, 2015 </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>INTRODUCTION </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">This Conflict Minerals Report for
Smith&nbsp;&amp; Wesson Holding Corporation (&#147;<B>SWHC</B>,&#148; &#147;<B>Company</B>,&#148; &#147;<B>we</B>,&#148; or &#147;<B>our</B>&#148;) is provided for the reporting period January&nbsp;1, 2014 to December&nbsp;31, 2014, and is presented
to comply with Rule 13p-1 under the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 (the &#147;<B>Rule</B>&#148;), and the Public Statement on the Effect of the Recent Court of Appeals Decision on the Conflict Minerals Rule issued by the Director of the Division of
Corporation Finance of the Securities and Exchange Commission on April&nbsp;29, 2014 (the &#147;<B>SEC Statement</B>&#148;). This Rule was adopted by the Securities and Exchange Commission (the &#147;<B>SEC</B>&#148;) to implement reporting and
disclosure requirements related to conflict minerals as directed by the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act (&#147;<B>Dodd-Frank Act</B>&#148;). The Rule imposes certain reporting obligations on SEC registrants whose
manufactured products contain tin, tantalum, tungsten, or gold (&#147;<B>3TG</B>,&#148; also defined by the rule as &#147;conflict minerals&#148;), and who have reason to believe that the products they manufacture, or contract to manufacture,
contain conflict minerals that are necessary to the functionality or production of those products. If the SEC registrant has reason to believe that any conflict minerals may have originated in the Democratic Republic of the Congo or an adjoining
country, or is unable to determine the country of origin of those conflict minerals, the SEC registrant is required to submit a Conflict Minerals Report to the SEC that includes a description of the measures it took to exercise due diligence on the
conflict minerals&#146; source and chain of custody. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>COMPANY BACKGROUND </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">We are one of the world&#146;s leading manufacturers of firearms. We manufacture a wide array of handguns (including revolvers and pistols), long guns
(including modern sporting, bolt action, and single shot rifles), handcuffs, and firearm-related products and accessories for sale to a wide variety of customers, including gun enthusiasts, collectors, hunters, sportsmen, competitive shooters,
individuals desiring home and personal protection, law enforcement and security agencies and officers, and military agencies in the United States and throughout the world. We are one of the largest manufacturers of handguns, modern sporting rifles,
and handcuffs in the United States and an active participant in the hunting rifle market. We are also a leading provider of shooting, reloading, gunsmithing, and gun cleaning supplies. We sell our products under the Smith&nbsp;&amp; Wesson<SUP
STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP>, M&amp;P<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP>, Thompson/Center Arms&#153;, Caldwell<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> Shooting Supplies, Wheeler<SUP
STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> Engineering, Tipton<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> Gun Cleaning Supplies, Lockdown<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> Vault Accessories,
Frankford Arsenal<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP>, Hooyman Saws&#153;, Golden Rod<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP>, and BOG-POD<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> brands. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">We manufacture our firearm products at our facilities in Springfield, Massachusetts; Houlton, Maine; and Deep River, Connecticut. We develop and sell our
shooting, reloading, gunsmithing, and gun cleaning accessories at our facility in Columbia, Missouri, the assets of which were acquired during the 2014 reporting period. We plan to continue to offer products that leverage the over 160 year old

 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>Conflict Minerals Report &#150; May 29, 2015 </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><B>REPORT </B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">This Conflict Minerals Report is provided for
the reporting period ended December&nbsp;31, 2014 in accordance with the Rule, the instructions to Form SD, and the SEC Statement. Please refer to the Rule, Form SD and the 1934 Act Release No.&nbsp;34-67716 for definitions to the terms used in this
Report, unless otherwise defined herein. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">In accordance with the Rule and the instructions to Form SD, SWHC undertook due diligence to determine the
conflict minerals status of any conflict minerals used in its products. In conducting its due diligence, SWHC implemented the Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development Due Diligence Guidance for Responsible Supply Chains of Minerals
from Conflict-Affected and High-Risk Areas (including the Supplements on Tin, Tantalum and Tungsten, and the Gold Supplement) (Second Edition OECD 2013) (&#147;<B>OECD Guidance</B>&#148;), an internationally recognized due diligence framework. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman"><U>Design of Our Due Diligence Measures </U></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Our conflict
minerals due diligence measures have been designed to conform with the OECD Guidance, as applicable for tin, tantalum, tungsten, gold and downstream companies (as the term is defined in the OECD Guidance), in all material respects. The Company does
not make direct purchases of raw ore or unrefined conflict minerals in the Covered Countries. We designed our due diligence measures to: </P> <P STYLE="font-size:12pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>
